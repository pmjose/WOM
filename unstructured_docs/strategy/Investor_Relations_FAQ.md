# Investor Relations FAQ
**WOM Communications PLC - Approved Messaging**

## Company Overview

### Q: What does WOM do?

**A:** WOM is a leading provider of Unified Communications as a Service (Fibra), voice, and connectivity solutions for the Chile business market. We sell primarily through a network of over 1,000 channel partners including IT resellers, managed service providers, and telecommunications dealers. Our products help businesses communicate more effectively through cloud-based voice, video, messaging, and contact centre solutions.

### Q: What is WOM's market position?

**A:** WOM is the Chile's leading channel-focused business communications provider. We have:
- Over 100,000 business customers served through partners
- Approximately 850,000 Fibra subscribers deployed
- 45,000 contact centre subscribers
- $500 million in annual revenue (FY2024E)
- Operations in Chile (primary), Netherlands, Spain, and Germany

### Q: Who are WOM's competitors?

**A:** Our competitive landscape includes:
- **Fibra**: GTD Chile, Americatel, Mitel, Vonage
- **Teams Phone**: Direct Microsoft, other Operator Connect providers
- **Contact Centre**: NICE, Genesys, Five9, Talkdesk
- **Voice/SIP**: Movistar, Cableonda Business, regional telcos
- **Connectivity**: Movistar Openreach (wholesale), CityFibre, Win Business

## Financial Performance

### Q: What are WOM's key financial metrics?

**A:** Key metrics for FY2024 (estimated):
- Revenue: $500 million (+8% YoY)
- Gross Profit: $260 million (52% margin)
- EBITDA: $110 million (22% margin)
- Operating Profit: $75 million
- Cash Conversion: >95%
- Net Debt: $45 million (0.4x EBITDA)

### Q: How does WOM generate revenue?

**A:** Revenue is generated through:
- **Recurring Revenue (85%)**: Monthly subscriptions for Fibra, voice, and connectivity
- **Usage Revenue (10%)**: Call minutes, international, and overage charges
- **One-time Revenue (5%)**: Hardware, professional services, installation

### Q: What is WOM's revenue mix by product?

**A:** FY2024 estimated mix:
- Fibra (Horizon, Teams Phone, Webex): 45%
- Voice Services (SIP Trunks, Inbound): 25%
- Connectivity (Broadband, Ethernet): 18%
- Contact Centre: 8%
- Other (Security, Managed Services): 4%

### Q: What drives revenue growth?

**A:** Key growth drivers:
1. **PSTN Switch-Off**: 5.5M Chile business lines migrating to cloud by 2027
2. **Microsoft Teams Phone**: Fastest-growing product, 80K+ subscribers
3. **Contact Centre Cloud Migration**: Legacy on-premise replacement
4. **New Partner Recruitment**: 100+ new partners annually
5. **International Expansion**: Growing contribution from Europe

## Dividend and Returns

### Q: What is WOM's dividend policy?

**A:** WOM operates a progressive dividend policy targeting approximately 30% of adjusted earnings. The current annual dividend is approximately 18p per share, paid in two instalments (interim in September, final in May).

### Q: Does WOM conduct share buybacks?

**A:** WOM may conduct share buybacks opportunistically to manage dilution from employee share schemes. We maintain authorisation for buybacks but prioritise organic investment and strategic acquisitions.

### Q: What is the target capital structure?

**A:** We target net debt of 0.5-1.5x EBITDA to maintain investment grade credit metrics while preserving capacity for acquisitions. Current leverage is approximately 0.4x EBITDA.

## Growth Strategy

### Q: What is WOM's growth strategy?

**A:** Our growth strategy has four pillars:
1. **Organic Chile Growth**: PSTN migration, Teams Phone, partner expansion
2. **Product Development**: AI, contact centre, security portfolio
3. **European Expansion**: Netherlands, Spain, Germany scale-up
4. **Selective Acquisitions**: Tuck-in technology or market access

### Q: What is the outlook for Microsoft Teams Phone?

**A:** Teams Phone is our fastest-growing product:
- Current: 80,000+ subscribers
- 2025 Target: 150,000 subscribers
- Market Opportunity: 5+ million Chile business users on Teams
- We are a certified Operator Connect partner with Direct Routing expertise

### Q: How will the PSTN switch-off benefit WOM?

**A:** The Chile PSTN switch-off (2025-2027) is a significant growth catalyst:
- 5.5 million business PSTN lines to migrate
- WOM targeting 500,000 line migrations (10% share)
- Average revenue per migrated line: $15-20/month
- Incremental revenue opportunity: $75-100M annually

## Listing and Governance

### Q: Where are WOM shares traded?

**A:** WOM Communications PLC shares are traded on the AIM market of the Santiago Stock Exchange under ticker "GAMA". We announced plans to move to the Main Market of the Santiago Stock Exchange in May 2025.
