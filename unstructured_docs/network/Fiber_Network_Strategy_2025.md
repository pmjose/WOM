# WOM Chile - Fiber Network Strategy 2025

## Executive Summary

WOM's 2025 network strategy focuses on expanding 100% fiber optic infrastructure across Chile's major departments while maintaining industry-leading speeds and reliability.

## Network Infrastructure

### Core Technology
- **FTTH (Fiber to the Home)**: 100% fiber optic, no hybrid networks
- **GPON/5G + GPON**: Gigabit-capable passive optical networks
- **Backbone**: High-capacity fiber rings connecting all regions

### Speed Tiers
| Plan | Download | Upload | Target Segment |
|------|----------|--------|----------------|
| 500 Mbps | 500 Mbps | 250 Mbps | Entry residential |
| 1500 Mbps | 1.5 Gbps | 750 Mbps | Standard residential |
| 2500 Mbps | 2.5 Gbps | 1.25 Gbps | Premium residential |
| 5000 Mbps | 5 Gbps | 2.5 Gbps | Power users/gamers |

### Enterprise Services
- Dedicated fiber links: 100 Mbps to 10 Gbps symmetric
- Point-to-point connections
- 99.99% SLA for enterprise customers

## Network Operations

### NOC (Network Operations Center)
- **Location**: Las Condes, Santiago
- **Staffing**: 24/7/365 monitoring
- **Capabilities**: Real-time network monitoring, automated alerts, incident management

### Key Performance Indicators
| Metric | Target | Q4 2024 Actual |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Network Uptime | 99.9% | 99.92% |
| Average Latency | <5ms | 3.2ms |
| Packet Loss | <0.1% | 0.02% |
| Mean Time to Repair | <4 hours | 2.8 hours |
| Customer Install Time | <3 days | 2.8 days (Santiago) |

### Redundancy
- Dual backbone paths between regions
- Battery backup at distribution nodes
- Diesel generators at major facilities
- Automatic failover for enterprise circuits

## Competitive Advantages

### vs. Claro/Movistar
- 100% fiber (no copper last mile)
- Symmetric upload/download speeds
- Lower latency for gaming
- No data caps

### vs. Cable Providers
- Higher speed consistency
- Better upload performance
- Future-proof infrastructure
